This order is for internal use only, and does not enlarge an officer’s civil or criminal liability in any way.
It should not be construed as the creation of a higher standard of safety or care in an evidentiary sense,
with respect to third-party claims. Violations of this directive, if proven, can only form the basis of a
complaint by this department, and then only in a non-judicial administration setting.

1. POLICY

Officers of the Pearisburg Police Department may use the issued BWC when interviewing
suspects of crimes, field sobriety tests, traffic stops, establishing probable cause for pedestrian
stops and detentions, or any situation, condition, or event having a potential for loss of life,
injury, or damage to property.

II. PURPOSE

To establish policy and guidelines for electronic data collection resulting from the use of body
worn audio/visual camcorders (BWC). It also serves to direct officers and supervisors in the
proper use and maintenance of the BWC. The puipose of the BWC is to provide an accurate
unbiased audio/visual record of enforcement related and non-criminal incidents that will enhance
criminal prosecutions and limit civil liabilities.

violation of this policy. Non-law enforcement business can include, but is not limited to:

handling a law enforcement incident and is in a lawful position when the taping
occurs.

B. Officers shall not use the BWC to record a particular person based soley on the person’s

race, color, age, sex, marital status, sexual orientation, gender identity, creed religion,

C. BWC will not be worn during wet weather as the unit is not weather proof.

B. Before going off-duty, officers will download all recordings to a designated secure
server/computer. The designated agency system administrator, Chief of Police or
Lieutenant will be contacted by the officer to copy any data that is deemed to be of
evidentiary value on a media storage device (i.e. CD-Rom, flash drive, etc.) and entered
into evidence. Officers will note in incident, arrest and related reports when video/audio
recordings were made during the incident in question. Other date not copied to disc will
remain on the secure server/computer for 90 days at which tune it shall be erased.

C. Data will not be released to another criminal justice agency for trial or other reasons

without having a duplicate copy made and returned to safe storage.

D. Data will not be released to any outside non-criminal justice entity, regardless of the

content, without explicit authority form the Chief of Police or his designee.

E. The media storage device shall be retained for 3 years or until the case is resolved or

evidence is released, whichever is longer, and then the media storage device or its
contents shall be destroyed.
